About me

Hi, I'm Adi — nice to meet you.

I'm a product designer in Chicago who's quietly convinced that good design is mostly good listening. I came to this work through language — how people mean things, not just what they say — and that lens never really left.

When I'm not untangling dense, regulated interfaces, I'm usually on a yoga mat, in the sand at Montrose Beach, or gently apologizing to a ceramic mug that did not survive the kiln.

QUIET WORK OF DESIGN

My Design Process

My background in language policy and pragmatics taught me to obsess over context — who's speaking, to whom, under what pressure. Turns out that's also the whole job of designing for high-stakes, regulated systems.

I like the hard, unglamorous problems: the dense screen, the edge case, the workflow everyone tolerates but no one loves. I bring research, patience, and a slightly chaotic Figma file — and I leave things calmer than I found them.
